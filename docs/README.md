# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management documentation! This repository contains detailed guides and best practices covering the entire project lifecycle at OctoAcme.

## Project Management Processes Overview

OctoAcme's project management processes are designed to ensure that work is delivered effectively through clear structure, defined roles, and repeatable workflows. The process begins with thorough project initiation, where teams validate the business need, align stakeholders, establish measurable goals, and produce essential artifacts like the Project One-pager and risk lists. Projects then move into a structured planning phase, focusing on breaking work into actionable increments, identifying risks and dependencies, and setting release milestones. Key artifacts—such as roadmaps, backlogs with acceptance criteria, and risk registers—provide the foundation for successful execution.

The organization operates with distinct core personas: Project Managers (PMs), Product Managers (PdMs), Developers, QA/Testing specialists, and Stakeholders, each with clearly defined responsibilities. PMs coordinate project timelines, risks, and documentation. PdMs drive product vision and prioritize work based on customer impact and measurable outcomes. Developers focus on implementing features, maintaining tests and documentation, and supporting technical quality, while QA/Testing ensures deliverables meet accepted standards. All roles collaborate iteratively, enabling fast feedback and continuous improvement.

Workflows rely heavily on structured boards (like GitHub Projects) for visibility and progress tracking, moving tasks through columns such as Backlog, In Progress, In Review, QA, and Done. Pull Requests are kept small and must reference their respective issues and acceptance criteria, with automated and manual quality checks before merging. Regular cadences—such as daily standups for progress tracking, weekly syncs for delivery and risk review, sprint demos, and scheduled retrospectives—create consistent communication, drive accountability, and ensure alignment.

Quality assurance is embedded across stages, with requirements for unit, integration, and end-to-end testing, as well as security scanning and manual QA for critical releases. Success metrics, burndown tracking, dashboards, and retrospective action items ensure that every project is measurable and constantly improved. Communication is transparent and regular, targeting different audiences through weekly status updates, incident communications, and monthly stakeholder briefings, supporting both proactive risk mitigation and rapid escalation when problems arise. This combination of roles, workflows, communication, and quality practices fosters a culture of psychological safety, data-driven decision making, and continuous learning.
